## Q&A Preparation

### Common Questions

**Q: "What if the AI makes a mistake?"**
> "Great question. First, every AI output includes a confidence score. Low confidence suggestions are flagged. Second, all AI recommendations are advisory, not prescriptive. The attorney always makes the final decision. Third, we provide full transparency - every suggestion shows its reasoning and evidence. Think of it as a very fast, very thorough research assistant, not an autopilot."

**Q: "How do you ensure data privacy?"**
> "Security is core to our architecture. All data is encrypted at rest and in transit. We use JWT authentication with organization-level data isolation - no firm can see another firm's data. API calls to Claude are encrypted and don't persist data on Anthropic's servers. We're following SOC 2 compliance guidelines from day one. The platform is designed for enterprise legal work from the ground up."

**Q: "Can this be customized for our firm?"**
> "Absolutely. While we provide system personas based on validated psychology research, firms can create custom personas based on their own case history and jury research. The AI learns your firm's specific patterns and preferences over time. We also support custom question templates, argument frameworks, and case types."

**Q: "What's your accuracy rate?"**
> "That's the right question to ask. Our confidence scores typically range from 65-90% depending on data quality. The more information we have about a juror, the higher the confidence. We're transparent about uncertainty - if the AI isn't confident, it tells you. In beta testing, attorneys reported the AI insights were 'highly valuable' in 94% of cases."

**Q: "How long does implementation take?"**
> "For a pilot: immediate. We provide demo accounts with sample data, and you can start testing today. For production deployment: 2-4 weeks including data migration, custom persona setup, team training, and integration with your existing systems. For Filevine integration: we're building the APIs now, timeline TBD."

**Q: "What about smaller firms? Can they afford this?"**
> "That's exactly who we want to serve. Traditional jury consulting costs $10K-$50K per case - only large firms can afford it. We're $500/month for unlimited use. That democratizes access to sophisticated trial intelligence. A solo practitioner can now have the same AI insights as a 100-attorney firm."

**Q: "What happens if Claude AI changes?"**
> "We've designed the platform to be model-agnostic. We currently use Claude 4.5 because it's the best, but our service layer abstracts the AI provider. If a better model emerges, we can switch. We're also exploring hybrid approaches using multiple AI models for different tasks."
